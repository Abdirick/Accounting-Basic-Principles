Basic Accounting Concepts

1. Assets (Things the company owns that have value)

Debit to increase | Credit to decrease

Type Examples Debits Credits
Current Assets - Cash
- Accounts Receivable
- Inventory
- Prepaid Expenses
- Short-term Investments		 Increase Decrease
Non-Current Assets - Property, Plant, and Equipment
- Long-term Investments
- Intangible Assets
- Vehicles
- Land		 Increase Decrease
2. Liabilities (What the company owes)

Credit to increase | Debit to decrease

Type Examples Debits Credits
Current Liabilities - Accounts Payable
- Short-term Loans
- Accrued Expenses
- Taxes Payable
- Current Portion of Long-term Debt		 Decrease Increase
Non-Current Liabilities - Long-term Debt
- Lease Obligations
- Pension Obligations
- Deferred Tax Liabilities		 Decrease Increase
3. Equity (Owners' claim after liabilities are paid)

Credit to increase | Debit to decrease

Examples Debits Credits
- Common Stock (Share Capital) Decrease Increase
- Retained Earnings (Accumulated Profits) Decrease Increase
- Additional Paid-in Capital (Over stock value) Decrease Increase
- Treasury Stock (Repurchased shares) Increase Decrease
- Dividends Paid Increase Decrease
4. Revenue (Income earned by the business)

Credit to increase | Debit to decrease

Examples Debits Credits
- Sales Revenue Decrease Increase
- Service Revenue Decrease Increase
- Interest Income Decrease Increase
- Dividend Income Decrease Increase
- Rental Income Decrease Increase
5. Expenses (Costs incurred to run the business)

Debit to increase | Credit to decrease

Type Examples Debits Credits
Operating Expenses - Salaries and Wages
- Rent
- Utilities
- Office Supplies
- Depreciation
- Advertising
- Insurance
- Repairs and Maintenance
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)		 Increase Decrease
Non-Operating Expenses - Interest Expense
- Taxes
- Loss on Sale of Assets		 Increase Decrease
